Nigerian Jollof rice

Savoring the essence of tradition in every grain. Jollof love on a plate.

Embark on a culinary journey to Nigeria with the tantalizing symphony of flavors that is Nigerian Jollof Rice. This iconic dish weaves together fragrant rice, vibrant tomatoes, and an exquisite blend of spices, promising a mouthwatering celebration of West African cuisine. Join us as we unlock the secrets to creating this beloved dish that transcends borders and captivates taste buds with every delectable bite. Get ready to elevate your kitchen to a hub of savory delight.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil or red palm oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3-4 large tomatoes, blended or diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, blended or diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 teaspoons thyme
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 2 teaspoons white pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 2-3 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional: 1-2 Scotch bonnet peppers for heat (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • Optional: 1 cup green peas or diced carrots for added texture and color

Steps:

  1. Rinse the rice: Place the rice in a bowl and rinse it thoroughly under cold water until the water runs clear. This helps remove excess starch.
  2. Blend or dice vegetables: Blend the tomatoes, red bell pepper, and optional Scotch bonnet peppers together until smooth. Alternatively, you can finely dice them.
  3. Sauté onions and garlic: In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they become translucent. Add minced garlic and grated ginger, cooking for an additional minute.
  4. Add blended vegetables: Pour the blended or diced tomatoes and peppers into the pot. Cook this mixture until the excess water evaporates, and the oil begins to separate from the tomato blend.
  5. Seasoning: Add thyme, curry powder, white pepper, bay leaves, and salt to the tomato mixture. Stir well to combine the flavors.
  6. Add broth and rice: Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth and bring the mixture to a boil. Stir in the rinsed rice, ensuring it's evenly coated with the tomato mixture.
  7. Simmer: Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid, and let the rice simmer until it's cooked and has absorbed the liquid. This usually takes about 20-25 minutes.
  8. Optional vegetables: If using, add green peas or diced carrots to the rice about 10 minutes before it's done cooking. This adds color and texture to the dish.
  9. Fluff and serve: Once the rice is cooked, fluff it with a fork to separate the grains. Remove the bay leaves. Serve the Jollof Rice hot and enjoy the burst of flavors!
