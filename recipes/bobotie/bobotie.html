Ingredients:

For Meat Mixture:

  • 1 kg ground beef or lamb
  • 2 slices of white bread, soaked in milk
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup raisins or sultanas
  • 2 tablespoons apricot jam
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

For the Topping:

  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric (for a golden color)

Steps:

  1. Rinse the rice: Place the rice in a bowl and rinse it thoroughly under cold water until the water runs clear. This helps remove excess starch.
  2. Blend or dice vegetables: Blend the tomatoes, red bell pepper, and optional Scotch bonnet peppers together until smooth. Alternatively, you can finely dice them.
  3. Saut√© onions and garlic: In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until they become translucent. Add minced garlic and grated ginger, cooking for an additional minute.
  4. Add blended vegetables: Pour the blended or diced tomatoes and peppers into the pot. Cook this mixture until the excess water evaporates, and the oil begins to separate from the tomato blend.
  5. Seasoning: Add thyme, curry powder, white pepper, bay leaves, and salt to the tomato mixture. Stir well to combine the flavors.
  6. Add broth and rice: Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth and bring the mixture to a boil. Stir in the rinsed rice, ensuring it's evenly coated with the tomato mixture.
  7. Simmer: Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid, and let the rice simmer until it's cooked and has absorbed the liquid. This usually takes about 20-25 minutes.
  8. Optional vegetables: If using, add green peas or diced carrots to the rice about 10 minutes before it's done cooking. This adds color and texture to the dish.
  9. Fluff and serve: Once the rice is cooked, fluff it with a fork to separate the grains. Remove the bay leaves. Serve the Jollof Rice hot and enjoy the burst of flavors!
